ORION: What It Is, What It’s Not
What ORION Is

ORION is a macro-signal investing system designed to detect structural change in the world and model how that change propagates through the global economy over time.

ORION does not predict prices.
It models causality.

At its core, ORION:

Ingests real-world events (policy, CapEx, conflict, technology)

Classifies them into structured event types

Propagates their impact through an explicit dependency graph

Surfaces second- and third-order beneficiaries before they are obvious

Produces human-readable, explainable investment signals

ORION is built for durable edge, not short-term excitement.

What ORION Is Not

ORION is not:

A trading bot

A price-prediction engine

A sentiment or hype detector

A black-box AI system

A day-trading or scalping tool

ORION exists to answer one question:

“What structural change is happening, how will it ripple through the system, and who benefits over time?”

Core Themes (Structural Pillars)

ORION organizes the world into persistent themes. Events attach to themes; themes anchor the graph.

AI

Compute, models, data centers, inference, automation, productivity amplification, and downstream demand for power, silicon, and infrastructure.

Energy

Electricity generation, grid capacity, fossil fuels, renewables, nuclear, storage, and energy security.

Defense

Military spending, conflict, deterrence, aerospace, cyber defense, surveillance, and national security supply chains.

Infrastructure

Physical build-out: data centers, grids, transportation, housing, water, industrial construction.

Semiconductors

Chips, fabs, equipment, materials, supply chains, and compute constraints.

Commodities

Raw inputs: copper, lithium, oil, gas, uranium, steel, cement, rare earths.

Geopolitics / Policy

Regulation, trade restrictions, sanctions, subsidies, industrial policy, and sovereign strategy.

(Themes are not mutually exclusive—nodes can belong to multiple.)

Event Types (With 1-Line Examples)

Each real-world change is normalized into an event object.

CapEx Expansion
Example: A hyperscaler announces $50B in new data center spending.

Regulatory Change
Example: Government introduces semiconductor export controls.

Supply Constraint
Example: Copper mine shutdown reduces global output.

Demand Acceleration
Example: AI model scaling drives surge in GPU demand.

Conflict / Geopolitical Shock
Example: Regional war disrupts energy shipping lanes.

Technological Breakthrough
Example: New process node materially improves chip performance per watt.

First-, Second-, and Third-Order Effects

ORION explicitly models order of impact.

First-Order Effects

Immediate, obvious, directly affected.

Example: AI CapEx → GPU manufacturers benefit

Second-Order Effects

Downstream enablers or bottlenecks.

Example: GPU demand → power utilities, grid equipment, cooling systems benefit

Third-Order Effects

Non-obvious structural beneficiaries.

Example: Grid strain → copper, transformers, construction firms, permitting services

ORION’s edge lives primarily in second- and third-order effects.

Lag: Timing Between Cause and Effect

Lag is the time delay between an event and its economic impact.

ORION treats time as a first-class variable, not an afterthought.

Lag Scenarios

Immediate (weeks):
Sanctions → commodity price dislocations

Medium (months):
CapEx announcement → equipment orders → supplier revenues

Long (years):
Policy shift → infrastructure build-out → commodity supercycle

Every edge in ORION’s graph has:

Direction

Impact strength

Expected lag

5 Things ORION Will Not Do

No Day Trading
ORION does not optimize for intraday or short-term price moves.

No Price Prediction
It does not forecast prices, targets, or exact timing.

No Black-Box AI Magic
Every signal must be explainable and traceable.

No Hype or Sentiment Chasing
Headlines without structural impact are filtered out.

No Execution Without Understanding
ORION informs decisions; it does not force trades.

Closing Principle

ORION is a thinking system before it is an investing system.

If a signal cannot answer:

What changed?
Why does it matter?
How does it propagate?
Who benefits, and when?

Then ORION ignores it.
